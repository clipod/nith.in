## Why This Site Exists

For years, I've been consuming knowledge from the internet — learning about distributed systems, experimenting with my homelab, tinkering with IoT, and thinking deeply about how engineering organizations work.

I never felt the need to write about it because I wasn't "inventing" anything new. Everything I know, I learned from someone else.

Then I realized: **that's exactly the point.**

## The Value of Synthesis

The most impactful engineering leaders I've worked with aren't the ones who invented new algorithms or published research papers. They're the ones who can take the overwhelming volume of information out there and turn it into clarity — for their teams, their organizations, and their stakeholders.

Synthesis is a skill. Curation is a skill. Taking five different perspectives on event-driven architecture and distilling them into a decision framework your team can actually use — that's real, valuable work.

## What I'll Write About

This site will cover the intersection of four areas I care deeply about:

- **Software Architecture** — Real-world patterns, trade-offs, and the decisions behind the diagrams
- **Engineering Leadership** — The journey from individual contributor to architect to director and beyond
- **AI & Agentic Systems** — Hands-on experiments with autonomous AI workflows and what they mean for enterprise
- **Homelab to Enterprise** — What I learn by tinkering at home and how it translates to production-scale systems

## The Homelab Advantage

One thing that sets my perspective apart: I maintain an active homelab. While many leaders at my level have moved away from hands-on technical work, I find that keeping my hands dirty makes me a better architect and a better leader.

When I evaluate a technology decision for my team, I've often already run it in my homelab. When I assess a new architecture pattern, I've frequently prototyped it at home first. This isn't about proving I can still code — it's about making better strategic decisions through direct experience.
